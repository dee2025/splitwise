# Splitwise Landing Page - Copy & Content Strategy

## Executive Summary

This landing page uses a **trust-first, clarity-focused** approach designed for young professionals and students who are first-time users. The messaging centers on **emotional relief** ("stop fighting") combined with **rational benefits** ("track fairly, settle easily").

**Key Design Principle:** Remove anxiety around money, not add urgency.

---

## Hero Section Copy Strategy

### Headline: "Stop fighting about who owes what"

- **Why this works:**
  - **Emotional hook:** "Fighting" captures the real problem (social tension, not just logistics)
  - **Specific:** "About who owes what" is concrete, not generic
  - **Negative ‚Üí Positive:** Implies solution (clear understanding of who owes what)
  - **Relatable:** Everyone has experienced this
  - **Short:** Eight words, easy to understand at a glance

- **What we avoided:**
  - ‚úó "Revolutionary expense tracking" (buzzword-heavy)
  - ‚úó "Manage shared expenses" (generic, not emotional)
  - ‚úó "The #1 bill splitting app" (unproven claim)
  - ‚úó "Share smartly, pay fairly" (clich√©)

### Subheading: "Split expenses with confidence. Track shared costs instantly, settle up fairly, and keep friendships intact."

- **Structure:** Problem statement + 3 benefits
  - "with confidence" = emotional state (safe, secure)
  - "instantly" = removes friction (real-time)
  - "fairly" = addresses trust concern (mathematical)
  - "keep friendships intact" = emotional payoff (not just money, but relationships)

- **Why this works:**
  - Addresses objection: "Will this ruin friendships?" ‚Üí "No, it keeps them intact"
  - Shows speed and fairness (top concerns)
  - Ends on emotional note (friendship preservation)

### CTAs: "Get Started Free" + "Download App"

- **"Get Started Free"** (Primary)
  - "Get Started" = action-oriented verb
  - "Free" = removes payment objection immediately
  - Combined: Removes friction at first step

- **"Download App"** (Secondary)
  - Offers alternative path
  - Immediate mobile option
  - Doesn't competing with primary (secondary styling)

- **Why this works:**
  - Addresses top objection (credit card friction)

## Copy Principles Applied Throughout

### 1. **Emotional First, Rational Second**

- ‚ùå "Automatic bill splitting" (rational)
- ‚úÖ "Stop fighting about who owes what" (emotional) + "automatic" (rational)

### 2. **Specific Over Generic**

- ‚ùå "Expense tracking"
- ‚úÖ "Track who paid for coffee in Bali with your friends"

### 3. **Problem-Solution Pairing**

- ‚ùå Just listing features
- ‚úÖ "mental math overload" ‚Üí "automatic calculation"

### 4. **Minimize Jargon**

- ‚ùå "Bi-directional sync" (technical)
- ‚úÖ "Sync instantly across devices" (user language)

### 5. **Make Every Word Count**

- ‚ùå "We provide a solution for efficient cost-sharing"
- ‚úÖ "Split bills fairly"

### 6. **Active Voice**

- ‚ùå "Expenses are tracked automatically"
- ‚úÖ "We track your expenses automatically"

### 7. **Restate Problem Before Solution**

- ‚ùå "Create groups, add members, log expenses"
- ‚úÖ "Confused about who owes what? Create a group..." (problem context first)

### 8. **Use Specific Numbers**

- ‚ùå "Lots of people use us"
- ‚úÖ "50k+ active users" (specific, verifiable)

### 9. **Recurring Benefit Emphasis**

- ‚ùå "Use once to split a trip"
- ‚úÖ "Use for trips, rent, dinners, events" (multiple use cases)

### 10. **No Hype Language**

- ‚ùå "Revolutionary," "Game-changing," "AI-powered"
- ‚úÖ "Simple," "Fair," "Clear"

## Tone & Voice Guidelines for Updates

### DO:

- ‚úì Be specific ("Log what you paid" not "Track expenses")
- ‚úì Use active voice ("We calculate" not "Calculations are made")
- ‚úì Be human ("Let's fix that" not "The solution provides")
- ‚úì Acknowledge emotion ("Stop fighting" not just "organize expenses")
- ‚úì Use conversational language ("What to trust" not "Trust pillars")

### DON'T:

- ‚úó Use buzzwords ("Disruptive," "Paradigm shift," "Synergy")
- ‚úó Make unsupported claims ("Best app" without proof)
- ‚úó Use corporate-speak ("Leverage," "Optimize," "Facilitate")
- ‚úó Exaggerate ("Transform your life" for an expense app)
- ‚úó Use passive voice ("Expenses will be tracked")

## Conversion Copywriting Checklist

Before publishing each section, verify:

- [ ] Headline is benefit-driven, not feature-focused
- [ ] Subheading adds qualification/detail
- [ ] CTAs remove friction (state what's required: $0, no CC, immediate access)
- [ ] Emotional + rational benefits both present
- [ ] No generic marketing language ("revolutionize," "transform")
- [ ] Specific examples or numbers where possible
- [ ] Active voice used consistently
- [ ] User perspective (not company perspective)
- [ ] Problem is stated before solution is offered
- [ ] Social proof present where relevant

This copy strategy balances marketing effectiveness with integrity, focusing on genuine product benefits and user emotional state rather than aggressive sales tactics.
